# Research notes (asset scan)

Three parallel research streams ran on 2026-04-27 to seed the concept work. This file captures the highest-value findings, distilled for ideation use.

## Comparable products and case studies

**Consumer teen apps that work**
- **BeReal:** mutual-reciprocity gate (you can't see until you post) flattens curation. Narrow but powerful mechanic.
- **Daylio:** mood + activity logging with pattern feedback. Data-driven loop shows causation between behaviour and feeling. Low friction.
- **Finch:** habit tracking via virtual pet care. Indirect activation: "feed your pet" not "track yourself." Habits compound into identity work without labelling.
- **Spotify Wrapped:** annual data viz as cultural artifact. Shareable identity claim where reflection is the byproduct.

**Education tools that failed**
- **Journey Together (MOE):** labelled as reflection, near-zero usage. Covertness paradox confirmed empirically.
- **SkillsFuture portal:** felt like admin task, no peer activation, individual portfolios with no audience.
- **Generic e-portfolios:** work moderately when teacher curates and parent loop closes (Seesaw). Fail when standalone.

**AI conversational tools**
- **Replika:** fills the trust void (no judgment, no consequences) but creates parasocial bonds. Privacy concerns.
- **Woebot:** good for crisis, inadequate for identity work. Teens want empathy, not techniques.

**Adjacent surprises**
- **Reddit r/toastme, r/AmIUgly:** anonymity enables honesty, but lacks long-term growth.
- **Notion templates teens share:** community norm + identity play + text-based depth. Fails when institutions create the same format.
- **OC creation in fandom (Wattpad, Tumblr):** distance through fictional projection makes self-reflection safe.

**Strong patterns:** reflection works when (1) not labelled as reflection, (2) peer activation present, (3) data loops first then narrative, (4) human in the loop, (5) anonymity or abstraction available.

## Trojan horse mechanics (resolves the covertness paradox)

Five overarching patterns from scanning games, social apps, and creative tools:

1. **Mutual reciprocity.** BeReal's gate, anonymous compliments, shared playlists. Honest signal flows when peers are equally exposed.
2. **Aesthetic ownership.** Mood boards, character design, playlist curation. Surface is theirs, not a form.
3. **Constraint-driven specificity.** Three words, one photo, binary choice. Vagueness is the enemy of honesty.
4. **Peer feedback loop.** NGL, OC communities, Wordle comments. The reflection happens when others reflect signal back.
5. **Intrinsic non-reflective hook.** Game, social moment, artifact. Reflection is the shadow, not the sun.

**Specific liftable mechanics**
- BeReal mutual posting gate
- Discord prompt bots (reaction-emoji polls)
- Anonymous compliment gates (NGL, Gas)
- Mood board / Pinterest-style curation as identity portrait
- OC (original character) creation as self-projection
- Playlist titles as narrative ("getting over him", "this is me but not really")
- Spotify Wrapped style annual recap
- Wordle / loadout share strings as casual identity claims
- AI character interview ("if your life was a movie")
- Constrained tabletop format ("I am... I am known for... My weakness is...")

**Resolution for the paradox:** the answer is not to hide reflection but to house it inside something the student already wants to do. Honest framing of an artifact whose reflective value is the byproduct.

## Identity and reflection pedagogy

**Frameworks worth lifting**
- **Marcia's identity statuses.** Most Sec 1-4 students are in moratorium or foreclosure. Developmental task is making moratorium safe and productive.
- **McAdams narrative identity.** Identity is a story being constructed. Sec 1-4 students struggle to weave episodic memories into a coherent arc. Direct fit for H1 (linguistic gap) and H2 (outward easier than inward).
- **Pennebaker expressive writing.** Structured prompts work better than blank pages. The magic is coherence-seeking, not venting.
- **Gibbs reflective cycle / Driscoll What-So What-Now What.** Explicit structure reduces blank-page anxiety and separates facts from feelings from meaning.
- **Implementation intentions (Gollwitzer).** "If [trigger] then [tiny action]" closes the action gap. Removes willpower.
- **Peer-mediated reflection.** Adolescents are more honest with peers than adults. One-to-one peer dialogue with structured questions beats group discussion.

**Liftable prompt styles**
- "Tell me about a time you surprised yourself."
- "What would your teammates say you bring to the group?"
- "What's something about yourself you've learned from a peer that surprised you?"
- "If you could fail safely at one thing, what would it be?"
- "If [specific situation] happens again, what's the smallest thing you'd do differently?"
- "What did you find out about yourself today?"

**Singapore-specific gap.** Official theory of change (LEAPS, CCE, Journey Together) emphasises pathway awareness over self-knowledge. Students get tools that list careers, not tools that build language for who they are. Concept design should prioritize the missing self-knowledge layer.

**Avoid** starting with freeform journaling at this age in a school context. Friction too high. Use structured sentence completion or peer dialogue formats instead.

## How this informs the three concepts

- **Concept 01 (Two Truths)** lifts: peer-mediated reflection, sentence completion prompts, implementation intentions, constraint-driven specificity, mutual reciprocity.
- **Concept 02 (CCA Wrapped)** lifts: BeReal capture mechanics, Spotify Wrapped synthesis ritual, Daylio data-loop feedback, Pennebaker structured prompt design, narrative identity framing.
- **Concept 03 (Postcards)** lifts: AI conversational interview, McAdams narrative identity, Gibbs / What-So What-Now What structure, future-self distancing, milestone anchoring.

Each concept passes the consequence-fear test by keeping artifacts off MOE servers and student-owned.
