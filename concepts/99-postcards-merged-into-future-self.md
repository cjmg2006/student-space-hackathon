# Postcards to Future Me — merged into Concept 03

This concept has been merged into [Concept 03 — Future Self Simulator](03-future-self-simulator.md).

## Why merged

Postcards (asynchronous letter-writing to a future self) and the Future Self Simulator (interactive conversation with a future self) were variations of the same underlying mechanic. The Simulator is the more developed form: it preserves the postcard as the artifact at the end of a Mode 1 (Talk to Future You) session, while adding interactivity, two more modes (Try a Day, Branching Path Explorer), and a stronger action-gap closure (if-then capture with notification reminder).

## Where the original idea lives now

- **The "letter to future self" experience** is preserved as the closing artifact of Mode 1 in Concept 03. After the conversation with future-you, the agent generates a postcard from the student's own words. The student picks an open date. The seal-and-reveal mechanic still applies.
- **The milestone-anchored framing** carries forward as a notification cadence option in Concept 03 (aligned to ECG checkpoints, end-of-term moments, post-competition).
- **The optional teacher response** carries forward as one of the relational activation layers for Concept 03.

## Why a separate concept didn't survive consolidation

Three concepts is a sharper exploration of the design space than four. The user signal (mobile-first agent simulation) pulled the future-self space toward Simulator. Postcards-as-its-own-thing was the weakest variant on the H3 (imagination) and H12 (action gap) axes because it lacked interactivity and a built-in next-step.

This file is kept as a redirect for anyone looking for the older Postcards concept.
