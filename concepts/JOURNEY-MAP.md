# Journey Map — Student Space

How the three solutions serve four personas across the moments that matter, grounded in the research synthesis.

## The framing

Three solutions × four mindsets × the trigger moments from research. The claim: the three solutions are temporally complementary (present, past, future) AND persona-complementary (each is the natural entry point for a different mindset). The system is one ecosystem the student traverses across years, with different doors for different students.

## The 4 mindsets (recap)

From the user-segmentation 2x2:

- **Eager but stuck** (high motivation, low articulacy) — wants to figure out future self but lacks language. Highest return on synthesis tools.
- **Self-directed** (high motivation, high articulacy) — already doing this work independently. Could be peer ambassadors.
- **Disengaged** (low motivation, low articulacy) — hardest to reach. Trojan horse designs matter most.
- **Capable but apathetic** (low motivation, high articulacy) — can articulate but doesn't see the point. Crisis or milestone activates them.

## The 3 solutions (recap)

- **01 Mirror Talk** — friends ask each other one good question, mirrors come back async. Anchored to H4, H6, H9, H10.
- **02b Say It Better** — voice-rant in, three articulated drafts out, pick and save. Anchored to H1, H5, H10, H11.
- **03 Future Self Simulator** — live a day in a possible future, debrief, postcard. Anchored to H3, H12.

## Triggers from the research

Grouped by frequency and intensity. Sources cited.

### Daily / weekly (high frequency, low intensity)

- **After CCA practice** — the canonical daily moment for reflection. Risk 3 evidence.
- **End of school day** — quiet moment to debrief.
- **Friend interaction (good or bad)** — H4: "40-50% of student issues surface through friends." Peer-mediated insight.
- **Quiet evening moment** — alone, thinking. The H1 "I feel good, I do this" gap surfaces here.

### Episodic (medium frequency, medium intensity)

- **Competition / performance / showcase** — concrete moment with high signal.
- **Test results / project feedback** — academic reflection trigger.
- **Class discussion or debate** — articulation pressure in real time.
- **Family conversation** — "what do you want to do?" advocacy moments.
- **Camp / retreat / overnight event** — bonded peer moments.

### Seasonal (low frequency, medium intensity)

- **End of CCA term** — natural reflection moment. Original CCA Wrapped target.
- **Subject combo decision (end of Sec 2)** — fork moment. Risk 3 inflection point.
- **CCA leadership election** — Risk 3 inflection point.
- **Start of Sec 1** — H9 trust-building period; reserved at first, opens up Term 2.

### Major (rare, high intensity)

- **Streaming decision** — Risk 3 inflection point.
- **JC vs poly decision (Sec 4)** — major fork.
- **DSA / scholarship interview** — H11 stakes-based context where students engage seriously.
- **Personal crisis** — H10 fear-of-consequences moment, where the wrong tool kills trust forever.
- **ECG career talks** — institutional triggers, often passive.

## Each solution as a different entry point

The system has three doors. Different mindsets use different doors first.

### Mirror Talk → the relational door

**Best entry point for:** Disengaged (a friend pulls them in), and Eager but stuck (everyday peer moments give them language they can't find alone).

**Why:** H4 (friends shape self-knowledge), H6 (relational), H9 (trust individuals). A Disengaged student won't open a reflection app, but they'll respond to a friend asking them a question through one. A friend invitation is the strongest pull this segment will accept.

**Trigger fit:** daily and weekly, peer-shared moments.

### Say It Better → the utility door

**Best entry point for:** Eager but stuck (their language gap is exactly what this fixes), and Capable but apathetic (when articulation is suddenly needed for an interview, application, or a parent asking the real question).

**Why:** H1 (linguistic gap), H11 (agency over surface). It works on the student's own terms, on demand. A Capable but apathetic student doesn't want a reflection app, but they want to nail the JC interview. The library happens to be the language for that moment.

**Trigger fit:** any moment, but especially episodic articulation pressure and major application contexts.

### Future Self Simulator → the curiosity-or-stakes door

**Best entry point for:** Self-directed (already exploring, this stretches the imagination), Disengaged (game-like Try a Day, no overt reflection framing), and Capable but apathetic (at fork moments where the stakes activate them).

**Why:** H3 (hard to see what's possible), H12 (action gap). The Try a Day mode is the trojan horse for Disengaged students (it feels like a game). For Capable but apathetic, fork moments like subject combo make this the right tool at the right time.

**Trigger fit:** seasonal forks, major decisions, curiosity-driven moments.

## Persona pathways through the system

The most likely journey for each persona across Sec 1-4.

### Eager but stuck (the primary segment)

Sec 1 Term 2 onwards: friend invites them to Mirror Talk → first mirror lands → they save it. Open Say It Better, articulate what the mirror revealed → first library entry. Sec 2: regular Mirror Talk + occasional Say It Better → library has 15-20 articulations across domains. End of Sec 2: subject combo coming up → opens Future Self → first Try a Day session → postcard sealed for end of Sec 3. Sec 3: deeper library, more confident articulations, occasional Future Self at decision points. Sec 4: JC interview prep → opens Say It Better library, rehearses answers from their own language. Mirror Talk continues throughout as everyday rhythm.

**Compounding effect:** highest. Library has years of articulations by Sec 4. Self-perception is built, not borrowed.

### Self-directed (peer ambassador)

Sec 1: discovers Future Self through curiosity, uses sporadically. Sec 2: starts inviting friends to Mirror Talk, becomes a node in the friend graph. Sec 3: uses Say It Better occasionally, as a refinement tool, not a discovery tool. Sec 4: Future Self for stretch goals (testing scholarship paths, Oxbridge applications).

**Compounding effect:** they help others compound more than they need it themselves. They're the gravity that pulls Disengaged friends in.

### Disengaged (the hard-to-reach)

Sec 1: doesn't engage with anything. Sec 2: a friend (a Self-directed peer) sends them a Mirror question. They reply. They get a mirror back. Save it. Sec 2 end: a friend shows them Try a Day as a game ("you'll like this, it's like Stardew Valley"). They play. They commit to one tiny experiment. They actually try it. Sec 3: occasional engagement, mostly through friends. Say It Better unlikely unless an articulation crisis hits.

**Compounding effect:** modest, and only if friends keep pulling them in. The system has to survive them being absent for months.

### Capable but apathetic (the fork-driven)

Sec 1-2: nothing. Sec 3 subject combo decision: Future Self Branching Path Explorer (when shipped) becomes useful. They try it because the stakes are real. Sec 4 DSA interview: Say It Better becomes the rehearsal tool. They articulate before the interview. Maybe Future Self once more before JC choice.

**Compounding effect:** sparse. They use the tools transactionally at moments that matter. The system has to deliver value in single sessions, not assume continuity.

## Cross-solution flows

The artifacts each tool produces feed the others. This is the ecosystem advantage.

**Mirror → Library → Simulation.** A friend mirror ("you stayed back to help Tasha — she didn't ask, you just saw her struggling") gets saved. The student opens Say It Better and articulates: "I'm someone who notices when others struggle." Months later, they open Future Self, pick "teacher" or "social worker" — the simulation seeds from this articulation. The postcard from future-you references "you noticed when Tasha was struggling that one practice."

**Simulation → Library → Mirror.** A Future Self session surfaces an instinct ("you like running things"). Saved as a Say It Better library entry. Next week, the student asks a friend through Mirror Talk: "what's something you've seen me run that I might not realize?"

**Library → Application context.** Across all three: every saved artifact (mirror, articulation, postcard) accumulates into a personal narrative archive. By Sec 4, this archive is the foundation for any application context (DSA, scholarship, JC interview). Out of explicit project scope, but inevitable as a side effect.

## Design implications

1. **All three are needed for full persona coverage.** No single tool reaches all four mindsets. Mirror Talk reaches Disengaged via friends. Say It Better reaches Capable but apathetic via stakes. Future Self reaches all via curiosity or forks. Skipping one leaves a persona under-served.
2. **Different doors for different students.** Onboarding shouldn't force a single entry. New users should be able to discover whichever solution matches their current need. The marketing/positioning of each tool needs to speak to a different mindset.
3. **The ecosystem flow is the long-term value.** Each solution standalone is useful. The artifacts compounding across solutions is what builds self-perception over years. This is the case for one-app vs three-apps: only one app makes the compounding visible to the student.
4. **Risk 3 (reactive vs reflective) is partially solved by the three-solution split.** Mirror Talk = high-frequency low-stakes (the muscle). Future Self = milestone-anchored (the moment). Say It Better = on-demand bursty (whenever needed). The two-mode-design Risk 3 calls for is actually a three-mode design.
5. **H9 trust-building (Sec 1 Term 1) is hard for any tool.** Future Self is probably the safest entry point for a brand-new Sec 1 student because it requires no peer trust and no past experience to articulate. Mirror Talk needs friend graph (takes a term to form). Say It Better needs something to articulate (takes weeks to accumulate). The new-Sec-1 onboarding should default to Future Self as the first taste.
6. **The "magic moments" are different per persona.** Eager but stuck has the magic in Say It Better's third-version reveal. Disengaged has the magic in the Future Self postcard or a friend's mirror. Self-directed has the magic in being asked to mirror others (giving). Capable but apathetic has the magic in a successful application that pulled from their library. Marketing should lead with the right magic moment for each segment.

## Test priorities

Given the journey map, the highest-value first test is:

**Run Mirror Talk + Say It Better for one cohort of Sec 2/3 students for 6 weeks**, with a single Future Self session at week 4 as the milestone anchor. Mix the cohort: 3 Eager but stuck, 2 Self-directed, 2 Disengaged, 1 Capable but apathetic.

Measure: did each persona engage with at least one solution within the first 2 weeks? Did the Disengaged students' engagement persist past week 3? Did Self-directed students invite others into Mirror Talk? Did the Future Self session at week 4 produce articulations that fed back into the library? At week 6, can each student name one thing they understand about themselves now that they didn't at the start?

If the journey map holds, all four personas should show movement, even if at different rates and through different doors.
