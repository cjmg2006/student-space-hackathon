# Concept cards

Three concepts, three cards. Each card is the elevator-pitch version of the corresponding prototype.

---

## 01 — Mirror Talk

> A mobile ritual where friends ask each other one good question and reflect each other back, asynchronously.

**How it works.** You pick friends who saw you today. They each get one question about you. They reply privately, in their own time. Their mirrors arrive in your library — sometimes hours later, sometimes the next day.

**The magic moment.** A new mirror lands with a soft chime of light, and you see yourself through someone else's eyes for the first time.

**Anchored to.** H4 (friends shape 40-50% of self-knowledge), H10 (fear of consequences). The disclosure surface is closed-friend-group, not institutional.

**File.** `01-mirror-talk.html`

## 02b — Say It Better

> A voice-rant-to-articulation tool that gives you back your own meaning, more clearly.

**How it works.** You ramble about something that happened. The app returns three drafts of what you meant — as you said it, as a claim about you, as a story. You pick the one closest to true, edit it, tag it, and save it. Over time the library compounds: a private dictionary of self.

**The magic moment.** The third version lands, and you think "yes, that's what I meant, but I couldn't say it."

**Anchored to.** H1 (the gap is linguistic, not experiential). Naming is the upstream act of identity. Self-perception, advocacy, decision-making, all flow from articulation.

**File.** `02b-say-it-better.html`

## 03 — Future Self Simulator (Try a Day)

> Step into a possible future. Live a Tuesday in it. Make choices. See how it goes.

**How it works.** You pick a future role. The simulation drops you into one day in that life: five moments from morning to evening, each with three choices. Future-you reacts to every choice with what actually happened. At day's end, future-you turns to you and asks which moment lit you up. Your answer branches the postcard you take home and the one tiny experiment you commit to trying this week.

**The magic moment.** Future-you turns to face you in the lamplit studio at 8:30 PM and asks the real question.

**Anchored to.** H3 (hard to see what's possible) + H12 (action gap). Imagination of possible selves is a learnable skill. The gap between "I can't see a future" and "one tiny thing to try" is the highest-leverage intervention in the problem space.

**File.** `03-future-self-simulator.html`
