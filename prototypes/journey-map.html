Three solutions, four mindsets, the moments that move them.

A map of how 01 Mirror Talk, 02b Say It Better, and 03 Future Self Simulator serve different student personas across the trigger moments surfaced in research. The three solutions form one ecosystem with three doors. Different mindsets enter through different doors.

03 — The three doors

Each solution is the entry point for a different mindset.

No single tool reaches all four personas. Each is the natural first door for a different mindset. Knowing this lets us position each solution distinctly, even if they live inside one app.

01 — RELATIONAL DOOR

Mirror Talk

The Disengaged student won't open a reflection app. But they will respond to a friend asking them a real question through one. A friend invitation is the strongest pull this segment will accept.

Best entry for

Disengaged Eager but stuck

Anchored in

H4 (friends shape 40-50% of self-knowledge), H6 (relational), H9 (trust individuals not institutions), H10 (fear of consequences).

02b — UTILITY DOOR

Say It Better

The Capable but apathetic student doesn't want a reflection app. But they want to nail the JC interview. The library happens to be the language for that moment. Articulation pressure is the activation.

Best entry for

Eager but stuck Capable but apathetic

Anchored in

H1 (the gap is linguistic, not experiential), H5 (past launchpad), H10 (fear of consequences as mitigation), H11 (agency over surface).

03 — CURIOSITY DOOR

Future Self

The Try a Day mode is the trojan horse for Disengaged students; it feels like a game. For Capable but apathetic, fork moments make this the right tool at the right time. For Self-directed, it's pure curiosity stretching.

Best entry for

Self-directed Disengaged Capable but apathetic

Anchored in

H3 (hard to see what's possible), H12 (action gap). Plus H1 and H5 secondary.

05 — How artifacts compound

The library that grows across solutions.

Each tool produces an artifact. Each artifact becomes input for another tool. This is the ecosystem advantage and the case for one app.

A friend's mirror becomes a saved articulation. The articulation seeds a future-self simulation. The simulation's postcard returns as another saved articulation. The library, over years, suggests questions to bring back to friends. Different mindsets enter through different doors but all eventually traverse the loop.

06 — Design implications

What this means for the v1.

Six implications drawn from the journey map. Each one is a decision the team can make ahead of prototyping.

01

All three are needed for full coverage.

No single tool reaches all four mindsets. Skipping one leaves a persona under-served. Mirror reaches Disengaged via friends. Say It Better reaches Capable via stakes. Future Self reaches all via curiosity or forks.

02

Different doors for different students.

Onboarding shouldn't force a single entry. New users discover whichever solution matches their current need. Marketing the three doors separately, even within one app, is the way to reach all four mindsets.

03

The ecosystem flow is the long-term value.

Each solution is useful standalone. The artifacts compounding across solutions builds self-perception over years. This is the strongest case for one app over three: only one app makes the compounding visible to the student.

04

Risk 3 splits into a three-mode design.

Mirror Talk = high-frequency low-stakes (the muscle). Future Self = milestone-anchored (the moment). Say It Better = on-demand bursty. The two-mode design Risk 3 calls for is actually three modes, one per solution.

05

Future Self is the safest first taste for Sec 1.

H9 trust-building is hardest in Sec 1 Term 1. Mirror Talk needs friend graph (a term to form). Say It Better needs accumulated experience. Future Self requires neither. New-Sec-1 onboarding should default to it.

06

Magic moments differ per persona.

Eager finds magic in Say It Better's third version. Disengaged in a friend's mirror or a postcard. Self-directed in being asked to mirror others. Capable in a successful application that pulled from their library. Lead with the right magic for each segment.
